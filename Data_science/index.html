Project workflow
From raw CSV → cleaned dataset → EDA → ML model → explainability.
Data acquisition & cleaning
From 2024 earnings to a clean police overtime dataset

Starting from employee_earnings_report_2024.csv, we filter Boston city employees, isolate Boston Police officers, drop noisy/missing columns, and impute remaining gaps to produce cleaned_police_overtime_data.csv with 14,057 rows and 0 missing values.

Notebooks: Boston_Police_Overtime.ipynb Tech: pandas · scikit-learn
Exploratory analysis
Visualizing pay patterns and extreme overtime

We explore distributions of regular and overtime pay by department, identify the top 10 highest-paid BPD officers, and inspect regular vs overtime scatter plots and correlation heatmaps to understand where overtime is concentrated.

Dashboard: dashboard.html Notebooks: data_visulization.ipynb
ML & explainability
Predicting overtime from pay components

Using regular, retro, injury, detail and other pay as features, we train XGBoost, Random Forest and Linear Regression regressors to predict overtime. XGBoost achieves R² ≈ 0.82 and MAE ≈ $2,756, explained using SHAP.
